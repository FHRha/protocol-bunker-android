# Game Rules

## What kind of game is this

Protocol: Bunker is a game about survival, discussion, and choice.

In the story, a group of people reaches a bunker, but there are fewer places inside than there are players. Because of that, players have to argue, persuade, and vote to decide who gets in and who stays outside.

The main idea is simple:
- not everyone can be saved;
- every character has strengths and weaknesses;
- the survivors are the ones the group considers the most useful.

## Goal of the game

Your goal is to make sure your character remains among the survivors by the end of the game.

To do that, you need to:
- reveal your cards wisely;
- explain why you are useful;
- take the catastrophe and current conditions into account;
- participate in the discussion;
- survive the votes.

## What each player gets at the start

At the beginning of the game, each player receives a set of character cards.

Usually this includes:
- profession;
- biology;
- health;
- hobby;
- baggage;
- fact;
- special condition.

Character cards start face down. Other players do not know everything about your character right away.

In addition, a catastrophe card is revealed at the start of the game. It defines the overall survival scenario. Bunker cards are also placed nearby and will be revealed as the game goes on.

## How the game works

A game consists of 5 or more rounds.

Each round follows the same basic structure:
1. bunker exploration;
2. card reveal;
3. voting.

## Phase 1. Bunker exploration

At the beginning of the round, one bunker card is revealed.

This card gives new information about the bunker itself and affects the discussion. Because of that, the survival conditions become clearer over time, and players have to rethink their arguments.

## Phase 2. Revealing cards

After that, players take turns revealing one character card each.

When a player reveals a card, they should briefly explain why their character still deserves a place in the bunker.

Other players may:
- ask questions;
- clarify details;
- argue;
- compare revealed traits between characters.

This is the stage where it gradually becomes clear who is truly useful for survival and who looks weaker compared to the others.

## Phase 3. Voting

After the discussion, voting begins.

Players nominate candidates for exile, and then a secret vote takes place.

In the basic version:
- everyone takes part in the discussion;
- all non-exiled players vote;
- the player with the most votes is exiled.

After that, the next round begins.

## What matters in discussion

Discussion is the core of the game.

The winner is not simply the one with the “best cards,” but the one who can:
- reveal strengths at the right moment;
- explain why their character is useful;
- notice the weaknesses of others;
- adapt to the catastrophe and bunker conditions;
- convince the group.

Even a strong character can be eliminated early if they defend themselves poorly. And on the other hand, a questionable character can survive to the end if their player handles the discussion well.

## What special conditions are

Special conditions are separate cards that add extra effects, restrictions, or hidden objectives to the game.

They make each game more varied and can noticeably affect discussion, target choices, and the overall balance.

## What happens to exiled players

Exiled players do not always disappear from the overall process completely.

In the logic of Bunker 3.0, their role may still matter until the very end of the game, depending on the selected ending mode.

## Endgame modes

In Bunker 3.0, the game can end in several different ways.

### Basic mode

This is the simplest version of the ending.

After a series of rounds and votes, the winners are the players who remain among the survivors at the end of the game. In this mode, the main goal is simply to stay alive until the finale and convince the group that you deserve a place in the bunker.

### Rebirth mode

In this mode, it is not enough just to reach the end. The survivors must also include a pair capable of continuing the human race.

This means the group has to think not only about who is useful right now, but also about the future after the catastrophe. Sometimes this changes priorities: a character who seems very strong in the moment may be less important for long-term survival than someone better suited to rebuilding life afterward.

### Survival story mode

In this version, surviving the votes is not enough.

After the main selection, the survivors must still deal with additional conditions and prove that they can actually continue surviving. In this mode, compatibility between survivors, group stability, and the ability to handle the aftermath of the catastrophe become much more important.

### Fate of the exiled mode

In this mode, exiled characters also play an important role.

Their story does not end the moment they are voted out: it matters not only who remains in the bunker, but also what happens to those the group rejected. This mode makes the game feel more dramatic and changes how players view the consequences of each vote.

## What affects victory

In Protocol: Bunker, there is no single perfect set of traits that guarantees victory in every game.

A lot depends on:
- the catastrophe scenario;
- which cards have already been revealed;
- the composition of the remaining players;
- how well the characters fit together;
- how convincingly a player explains their usefulness.

Because of that, even a very strong character does not always make it to the end, while a seemingly weak or awkward one may survive if the group decides they are more important at that moment.
